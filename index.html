echocardiography patch

01

Pipeline

01
phantom

Scatterer generation

Digital cardiac phantoms converted into intensity-weighted acoustic scatterer maps. Up to 50,000 scatterers across a 10 cm × 10 cm field of view.

02
transmit

RF data simulation

64 diverging-wave transmissions at ±45° from a virtual source at −4 cm. JAX/XLA GPU engine generates raw RF at 10.8 MHz sampling.

03
reconstruct

Beamforming & imaging

Delay-and-Sum with Hanning receive apodisation. Coherent compounding, envelope detection, and 60 dB log-compression to produce B-mode sector images.

02

Technical stack

JAX / XLA
GPU-native RF simulation via jit() and vmap(). ~31× speedup over CPU baselines.
UltraSpy
DAS beamforming engine with GPU/CPU fallback, polar scan geometry, envelope detection.
MATLAB / MUST
Reference SIR-based simulation for cross-platform validation and benchmarking.
PyMUST
Standalone Python reimplementation of MUST for CPU-based RF generation and validation.
FPGA back-end
Disaggregated hardware architecture with flexible piezoelectric array on PDMS substrate.
OpenCV / SciPy
Image processing, bandpass filtering, and quality metrics — SSIM, SNR, CNR, FWHM.

04

Research context

Programme
PhD in Biomedical Engineering at the Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT), supervised by A/Prof Alex Qiang Chen, A/Prof Mahesh Raveendranatha Panicker, and Dr. Zainul (TRICOG, industry partner).
Scope
128-channel wearable multi-view echocardiography patch with a disaggregated architecture: flexible piezoelectric array on PDMS substrate, FPGA back-end for data acquisition, and laptop GPU for real-time image reconstruction.
